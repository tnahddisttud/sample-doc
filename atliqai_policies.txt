# AtliqAI HR Policies

AtliqAI is committed to building a transparent, inclusive, and high-performance workplace. This document outlines the policies and guidelines that govern employment, conduct, compensation, and well-being at AtliqAI. All employees are expected to read, understand, and adhere to these policies from their first day of joining.

---

## Employment & Onboarding

### Offer and Joining Formalities

Upon acceptance of an offer letter, candidates must complete the joining formalities within the stipulated date mentioned in the offer. The HR team will share a pre-joining checklist that includes submission of educational certificates, identity proof, address proof, previous employment documents, and a recent photograph. Failure to submit required documents within 7 working days of joining may result in withholding of the first salary disbursement.

### Probation Period

All new employees at AtliqAI are placed on a probation period of 6 months from the date of joining. During this period, either party may terminate the employment with a notice period of 15 days. Performance will be reviewed at the end of the 3rd and 6th month. Successful completion of probation leads to confirmation of employment, which will be communicated in writing by the HR department.

### Background Verification

AtliqAI conducts a mandatory background verification for all new hires. This includes employment history verification for the last 5 years, educational qualification checks, criminal record screening, and reference checks from at least two previous managers. The verification is carried out by a third-party agency. Employment is contingent upon satisfactory results, and any discrepancy found may result in immediate termination.

### Employment Types

AtliqAI recognises the following employment types: full-time employees, part-time employees, contractual staff, and interns. Full-time employees are eligible for all standard benefits described in this document. Part-time and contractual staff receive a pro-rated subset of benefits as defined in their contracts. Interns are governed by a separate internship policy and are not entitled to standard employee benefits unless explicitly mentioned.

## Working Hours & Attendance

### Standard Working Hours

The standard workweek at AtliqAI is 40 hours, spread across 5 days (Monday to Friday). Core working hours are 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM IST, during which all employees are expected to be available. Employees may flex their start and end times outside of core hours, subject to manager approval.

### Remote and Hybrid Work

AtliqAI follows a hybrid work model. Employees are expected to be present in the office at least 3 days per week unless their role has been explicitly designated as fully remote. Remote work days must be pre-approved by the reporting manager. Employees working remotely are held to the same productivity and availability standards as those working from the office.

### Attendance Tracking

Attendance is tracked through the AtliqAI HRMS portal. Employees must log in at the start of the workday and log out at the end. Failure to mark attendance for more than 3 consecutive days without prior intimation will be treated as absence without pay and may trigger an HR review. Regularisation of missed attendance must be requested within 7 calendar days.

### Overtime

AtliqAI does not mandate overtime. However, in exceptional project circumstances, employees may be requested to work beyond standard hours. Overtime for non-exempt staff will be compensated at 1.5x the standard hourly rate. Exempt salaried employees are not entitled to overtime pay but may receive compensatory off at the manager's discretion.

## Leave Policy

### Casual Leave

Every confirmed employee is entitled to 12 casual leaves per calendar year, credited at 1 leave per month. Casual leave can be availed for personal errands, minor illness, or unplanned absences. A maximum of 3 consecutive casual leaves can be taken at a time. Casual leaves cannot be carried forward to the next calendar year and lapse on December 31st.

### Sick Leave

Employees are entitled to 10 sick leaves per calendar year. Sick leave can be availed in case of illness, hospitalisation, or medical procedures. A medical certificate from a registered practitioner is mandatory for sick leave of more than 2 consecutive days. Unused sick leaves up to a maximum of 10 can be carried forward to the following year.

### Earned Leave

Employees accrue earned leave at the rate of 1.25 days per month, amounting to 15 days per year. Earned leave can be carried forward up to a maximum of 30 days. Accumulated earned leave beyond 30 days will be encashed at the end of the financial year at the basic salary rate. Earned leave must be applied at least 5 working days in advance and is subject to manager approval.

### Maternity Leave

Female employees who have completed at least 6 months of continuous service are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two live births. For the third child onwards, the entitlement is 12 weeks. Maternity leave can begin 8 weeks before the expected delivery date. The employee must submit a medical certificate indicating the expected date of delivery at least 4 weeks in advance.

### Paternity Leave

Male employees and non-birthing partners are entitled to 10 working days of paid paternity leave, to be availed within 6 months of the child's birth or adoption. Paternity leave is a one-time entitlement per child and cannot be split across multiple instances. Application must be made at least 2 weeks before the intended start date.

### Bereavement Leave

Employees are entitled to 5 working days of paid bereavement leave in the event of the death of an immediate family member. Immediate family includes spouse, children, parents, and siblings. For extended family such as grandparents, in-laws, and first cousins, 2 days of bereavement leave are available. Additional unpaid leave may be requested subject to manager discretion.

### Leave Without Pay

Employees who exhaust all available leave balances may apply for leave without pay (LWP). LWP must be approved by the reporting manager and HR. More than 10 days of LWP in a financial year may impact annual performance ratings and bonus eligibility.

## Compensation & Benefits

### Salary Structure

AtliqAI follows a cost-to-company (CTC) model. The salary structure comprises Basic Pay (40% of CTC), House Rent Allowance (20% of Basic), Special Allowance (variable), Provident Fund employer contribution (12% of Basic), and annual performance-linked bonus. Detailed salary breakdowns are provided in each employee's appointment letter.

### Salary Disbursement

Salaries are disbursed on the last working day of every month directly to the employee's registered bank account. Employees must ensure their bank account details are updated in the HRMS portal. Delays caused by incorrect bank information are not the liability of AtliqAI.

### Performance Bonus

AtliqAI conducts annual performance reviews in March. Employees who receive a rating of "Meets Expectations" or above are eligible for a performance bonus. The bonus ranges from 8% to 25% of the annual basic salary, depending on individual rating and company performance. Employees who have not completed 6 months of service by the review date are not eligible for the bonus in that cycle.

### Provident Fund

AtliqAI contributes 12% of the employee's basic salary towards the Employee Provident Fund (EPF) as per the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952. The employee contributes a matching 12% from their basic salary. The combined amount is deposited monthly with the EPFO. Employees can track their PF balance through the EPFO Member Portal.

### Health Insurance

All full-time employees and their immediate dependents (spouse and up to 2 children) are covered under a group health insurance policy with a sum insured of ₹5,00,000 per annum. The premium is fully borne by AtliqAI. Pre-existing conditions are covered after a 30-day waiting period. Employees may opt to enhance their cover or add parents as dependents at an additional premium, which will be deducted from their salary.

### Gratuity

Employees who have completed 5 years of continuous service are eligible for gratuity as per the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972. Gratuity is calculated as (Last Drawn Basic Salary × 15 × Years of Service) / 26. It is paid at the time of resignation, retirement, or death of the employee.

## Performance Management

### Review Cycle

AtliqAI follows a bi-annual review cycle. Mid-year reviews are conducted in September and annual reviews in March. Both reviews involve a self-assessment by the employee, followed by a manager assessment, and a calibration session with HR. Final ratings are shared with employees by the end of the review month.

### Rating Scale

Performance is rated on a 5-point scale: 1 - Needs Improvement, 2 - Partially Meets Expectations, 3 - Meets Expectations, 4 - Exceeds Expectations, 5 - Outstanding. Employees rated 1 for two consecutive review cycles will be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).

### Performance Improvement Plan

A Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) is a structured 60-day program designed to help employees address performance gaps. The PIP document outlines specific measurable goals, timelines, and support provided. If the employee successfully meets the PIP targets, they return to normal employment standing. Failure to meet PIP targets may result in termination of employment.

### Promotion Policy

Promotions at AtliqAI are merit-based and tied to the annual review cycle. To be eligible for promotion, an employee must have a minimum rating of 4 in the last two consecutive reviews and must have completed at least 18 months in their current role. All promotions must be approved by the department head and the HR leadership team.

## Code of Conduct

### Professional Behaviour

All employees are expected to maintain a high standard of professional conduct at all times, both within the office and while representing AtliqAI externally. This includes treating colleagues, clients, and vendors with respect, meeting deadlines and commitments, and maintaining confidentiality of company and client information.

### Dress Code

AtliqAI follows a smart casual dress code in the office. Employees are expected to dress appropriately and professionally. Torn, overly casual, or revealing clothing is not permitted. On client-facing days or formal meetings, formal business attire is expected. Employees working from home are expected to dress professionally for all video calls.

### Conflict of Interest

Employees must disclose any potential conflict of interest to their manager and HR as soon as it arises. A conflict of interest includes holding a financial interest in a competitor, having a personal relationship with a vendor or client, or engaging in activities that compete with AtliqAI's business. Undisclosed conflicts of interest are grounds for disciplinary action.

### Social Media Policy

Employees must not share confidential company information, client data, financial results, or product details on any public platform. Personal opinions expressed online must include a disclaimer that views are personal and not representative of AtliqAI. Employees found violating the social media policy may face disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## Anti-Harassment & Inclusion

### Zero Tolerance Policy

AtliqAI has a zero-tolerance policy towards harassment of any kind, including sexual harassment, bullying, discrimination based on gender, caste, religion, nationality, disability, or sexual orientation. Any form of harassment — verbal, physical, or digital — is a serious violation of company policy and will be dealt with swiftly and strictly.

### Internal Complaints Committee

AtliqAI has constituted an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) as required under the Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Act, 2013. The ICC comprises a Presiding Officer, two internal members, and one external member from an NGO. Employees may report harassment complaints directly to any ICC member or via the HR helpdesk. All complaints are treated with strict confidentiality.

### Complaint Resolution Process

Upon receiving a complaint, the ICC will acknowledge it within 3 working days and initiate an inquiry within 7 working days. The inquiry will be completed within 60 days. Both the complainant and the respondent will be given an opportunity to present their case. The ICC will submit its findings and recommendations to the management, who will take action within 30 days of receiving the report.

### Equal Opportunity Employment

AtliqAI is an equal opportunity employer. All recruitment, promotion, compensation, and training decisions are made solely on the basis of merit and business need, without regard to race, gender, religion, age, disability, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic. AtliqAI is committed to maintaining a diverse and inclusive workplace.

## Separation & Exit

### Resignation Process

Employees wishing to resign must submit a formal resignation letter through the HRMS portal or via email to their reporting manager with a copy to HR. The notice period is 30 days for employees in Band 1-3 and 60 days for employees in Band 4 and above. The notice period begins from the day the resignation is accepted by the manager.

### Notice Period Buy-Out

Employees who are unable to serve the full notice period may request a notice period buy-out, subject to management approval. The buy-out amount is calculated at the basic daily rate for each day of unserved notice. The amount will be deducted from the full and final settlement. AtliqAI also reserves the right to waive the notice period at its discretion.

### Full and Final Settlement

The full and final settlement (FnF) will be processed within 45 days of the employee's last working day. The FnF includes payment of pending salary, leave encashment of earned leaves (up to the carry-forward limit), reimbursement of pending expense claims, and deduction of any outstanding dues or advances. The relieving letter and experience certificate will be issued after the FnF is cleared.

### Exit Interview

All exiting employees are encouraged to participate in a confidential exit interview with the HR team. The purpose of the exit interview is to gather feedback on the employee's experience at AtliqAI, identify areas of improvement, and ensure a smooth transition. Participation is voluntary but strongly recommended.

## Grievance Redressal

### Raising a Grievance

Any employee who has a work-related grievance — including concerns about compensation, working conditions, interpersonal conflicts, or policy violations — may raise it formally through the HRMS portal under the "Grievance" section. Grievances may also be submitted in writing to the HR Business Partner assigned to the employee's department.

### Grievance Resolution Timeline

HR will acknowledge the grievance within 2 working days of submission. A resolution will be communicated within 15 working days. Complex grievances requiring investigation may take up to 30 working days. The employee will be kept informed of the status throughout the process.

### Escalation

If an employee is not satisfied with the resolution provided, they may escalate the matter to the Head of HR within 10 working days of receiving the resolution. The Head of HR's decision is final in matters of policy interpretation. Legal or statutory matters may be referred to appropriate external authorities.

## Data Privacy & Confidentiality

### Employee Data

AtliqAI collects and processes employee personal data solely for the purposes of employment management, payroll, compliance, and benefits administration. Employee data is stored securely and is accessible only to authorised HR personnel. Employees have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of their personal data by contacting the HR helpdesk.

### Confidentiality Obligations

All employees are required to sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) as part of their employment contract. The NDA covers trade secrets, client information, proprietary technology, financial data, and internal strategies. Confidentiality obligations remain in effect for 2 years after the termination of employment.

### Data Breach Reporting

Any employee who becomes aware of a potential data breach or unauthorised disclosure of company or client data must report it to the IT Security team and HR within 24 hours. Timely reporting is essential to contain the breach and meet regulatory obligations. Failure to report a known breach is a disciplinary offence.
